THE HIGHLANDERS FIRST ROBOTICS (2022)

2022

Robotic Subsystem Development

During the 2022 FIRST Robotics season, I focused on the design and prototyping of high-performance robotic mechanisms. I was responsible for developing the intake, shooter, and climber systems, which required balancing speed, reliability, and precision. We successfully fielded one developmental and one competition-ready robot.

2022 Robot - Intake and Shooter Detail

One of my primary technical accomplishments was the full design and manufacturing of a 1-stage chain-driven elevator. This subsystem was critical for the season's climbing challenge, and I oversaw its integration from initial SolidWorks sketches to final assembly.

Advanced Manufacturing & Competition

CNC Milling - Custom Gussets and Tubes

To create the custom components needed for our robot, I utilized an array of advanced machinery, including mills, lathes, band saws, and laser cutters. I generated custom G-code using Fusion 360 for high-precision 3-axis CNC operations on a Haas VF-2 and Tree Journeyman mill running LinuxCNC. This hands-on manufacturing was essential for producing the specialized gussets and tubes that formed our chassis.

Competition Action Shot - Scoring in the Hub

Our dedication led to significant competitive success, winning Autonomous, Engineering Inspiration, and Innovation in Control awards across various regionals. We were finalists at Oklahoma, Colorado, and Utah, and eventually won the Entrepreneurship Award in the Hopper Division at the Houston World Championships.
