About Experiences Projects

THE HIGHLANDERS FIRST ROBOTICS (2023)

2023

Elite Competition Engineering

In the 2023 season, I served as the CAD Lead for Team 4499, The Highlanders. Our goal was to design and manufacture a world-class robot capable of competing at the highest levels of the FIRST Robotics Competition. My leadership involved overseeing the full design lifecycle for two functional robots, ensuring parts were manufactured to exacting tolerances of ±0.0005 inches.

2023 FRC Robot - Full Assembly View

A cornerstone of our 2023 design was a 3-stage cascade rigged, belt-driven arm. I engineered this subsystem to be entirely hot-swappable, allowing for rapid repairs during high-intensity competition. My work spanned the design, manufacturing, and assembly of the arm, wrist, A-frame, and chassis, integrating Onshape for collaborative CAD and Fusion 360 for custom tool path generation.

Innovation & Performance

Custom Clutch Gear Detail

To solve a critical issue with belt skipping under high torque, I designed and manufactured a custom clutch gear. This mechanical innovation significantly improved the reliability of our scoring mechanisms. Our manufacturing process was entirely in-house, utilizing manual and CNC mills, followed by powder coating and Cerakote finishes to ensure durability.

Championship Banners - Winners of Utah, Colorado, and Rocket City Regionals

The results of our 50+ hour work weeks were historic for the team. We were 1st place and Excellence in Engineering winners at the Utah Regional, and 1st place with Autonomous Awards at both the Colorado and Rocket City Regionals. At the Houston World Championships, we ranked 7th in the Johnson Division, placing us among the top 1% of robotics teams worldwide.
