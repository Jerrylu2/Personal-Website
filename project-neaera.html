About Experiences Projects

NEAERA CONSULTING

2023 - 2024

Autonomous Flare Deployment Systems

Working with Neaera Consulting, I was tasked with the end-to-end development of a MIRV (Mobile Integrated Robotic Vehicle) garage and intake system. This project was developed for the Virginia Tech Transportation Institute (VTTI) and the USDOT to create a platform capable of autonomously deploying non-incendiary flares in various patterns for roadside safety.

The MIRV Chassis and Intake Assembly

I was responsible for the manufacturing of the primary structures, working with 5052 aluminum sheet metal, 6061 aluminum square tubing, and polycarbonate sheets. Through multiple iterations, I refined the design of the "garage" where the flares are stored and the intake mechanism that feeds them into the deployment unit.

Precision Manufacturing & Integration

CNC Machining - Haas VF-2 Custom G-Code

To manufacture the custom components, I created intricate G-code using Fusion 360 for a Haas VF-2 CNC mill. This allowed for high-precision machining of the aluminum chassis parts. I also utilized a manual brake to bend sheet metal components to exact specifications.

Final Finished MIRV product in testing

A key part of the integration process involved 3D modeling the spare tire bay of transport vehicles to ensure the MIRV system could be deployed seamlessly from existing fleet hardware. The final product successfully met the requirements of VTTI and USDOT, providing a robust, autonomous solution for highway safety operations.
