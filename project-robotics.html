Bio-Inspired Origami Robotics

During my time as a Research Assistant at the Colorado State University Adaptive Robotics Laboratory, I explored the intersection of mechanical engineering and soft robotics. My work centered on prototyping a novel origami-inspired robot designed to dynamically change its physical shape to navigate varied environments.

Yoshimura Folding Pattern - Prototype Demo

I designed and implemented a Yoshimura folding pattern that utilized configurable locks on each joint. This allowed the robot to transition between rigid and flexible states. Using SolidWorks, I developed complex 3D printed joints made from a combination of PLA and TPU, leveraging the material properties of each to provide both structural support and the necessary elasticity for folding.

Rapid Prototyping & Control

Multi-Material 3D Printing Detail (TPU and PLA)

Achieving consistent results required deep technical knowledge of additive manufacturing. I utilized PrusaSlicer to tune print settings across a variety of hardware, including Bambu and Prusa 3D printers, ensuring that the interlocking origami facets functioned without binding. To bring the mechanisms to life, I wired and programmed an Arduino Uno R3 to control the actuators that drove the folding motions.

Claw-Shaped Adaptive Robot in Motion

The culmination of this research was the development of a claw-shaped robot capable of single cable-driven adaptive motion. This project resulted in me being named as the second author on a research paper submitted to the IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA), a testament to the project's technical novelty and contribution to the field.
