ILLINI SOLAR CAR

2024 - Present

Structural Optimization & Integration

As the Structures Project Lead for the Illini Solar Car team, I focused on optimizing critical mechanical components for the 2025 Formula Sun Grand Prix (FSGP) vehicle. My primary objective was to reduce the overall vehicle mass while ensuring uncompromising structural integrity and safety for the driver.

Render of the optimized Rear Brake Caliper Mount

I led the redesign of the rear brake caliper mount, a component requiring precise synchronization with the brake disc. By identifying the exact forces and interactions acting on the mount, I was able to transition the design into NX for advanced modeling. This redesign focused on streamlining the interface and removing redundant material without sacrificing load-bearing capacity.

Advanced Analysis & Manufacturing

Ansys Static Structural Analysis - Stress Distribution

To validate the integrity of the new mount, I performed extensive calculations using Python to determine forces, bolt pretension, and applied regulation load conditions. I then setup and ran static structural analysis and topology optimization in Ansys. These simulations confirmed that the part would maintain a 1.5 Factor of Safety (FS), meeting all driver safety regulations while achieving a 12% weight reduction compared to previous versions.

The final CNC-machined 7075 Aluminum part

Beyond the caliper mount, I am currently leading the design and integration for the vehicle's canopy latch system. My focus remains on manufacturability; for the caliper mount, I produced a final part using 7075 Aluminum that was designed to be manufactured entirely in-house, significantly saving costs and lead time for the team.
